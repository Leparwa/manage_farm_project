EXPENDITURE ANALYSIS

milk production

The production of milk requires that the cow be in lactation, which is a result of the cow having given birth to a calf. The cycle of insemination, pregnancy, parturition, and lactation, followed by a "dry" period of about two months of forty-five to fifty days, before calving which allows udder tissue to regenerate. Having four ranches the farm is able to produce amount of milk that translate to about 98% of the total production

Animals checkup expenses

Animal checkup expenses ,the farm is able to monitor the whole development of the cow from the time it a calf until it milk production stage,various expenses are incurred which include vaccination and other essential methodologies that ensure that the livestock are in proper health for proper and quality production

98%
milk production expenses
Milk production depends on breeding, feeding and management of animals. It constitute 65-70 percent of the cost of milk production. The farmer must asses the amount of milk that he/she produces on a daily basis and keep it on record,the production expense helps the farmer to be able to determine whether the farm is making a loss or profit.This might be due to the reason that transaction costs for collecting milk from the farms is high which in turn inflates milk price.
80%
Food Cost

Cost of feed have the highest share in the farm. We feed different feed types which includes Hay, concentrates, grasses and other common feed. High share of feed cost goes to concentrates followed by green grass and hay. Concomitantly, the cost goes to concentrate followed by hay and green grass. Moreover, we disburse 3% more mean feed cost per cow per year . This is due to the economies of scale. The concentrate feed type includes oil seed cake, molasses, wheat bran, by products of local beer and others.
